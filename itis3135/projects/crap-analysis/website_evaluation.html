Evaluating contrast, repetition, alignment, and proximity to understand what works—and what fails.

Website Evaluations

Below are two websites evaluated using the CRAP design principles: Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, and Proximity. One example shows a well-designed site, and the other highlights common problems when these principles are ignored.

Apple (Well-Designed Example)

Apple’s homepage is a clean, modern marketing site for their hardware and services. It focuses on a few key products at a time, using strong visual hierarchy and lots of white space to guide your attention.

  • Contrast: High contrast between product images, bold text, and clean backgrounds — very readable.
  • Repetition: Consistent typography, spacing, and layout patterns throughout. Feels cohesive.
  • Alignment: Elements align neatly in columns/rows; nothing floats randomly.
  • Proximity: Related items (heading, description, button) are grouped closely while different sections have good spacing.

LINGsCARS (Chaotic Example)

LINGsCARS is known for a loud, cluttered, and chaotic aesthetic. It overloads the user with conflicting visuals, making navigation and focus difficult.

  • Contrast: Colors clash and there’s inconsistent readability — loud backgrounds with bright text that competes rather than guides.
  • Repetition: Too many different fonts, sizes, and styles — no consistency from section to section.
  • Alignment: Elements are scattered, misaligned, and overlapping — hard to scan or predict layout flow.
  • Proximity: Unrelated content is crammed together, while related items can be far apart — creates confusion and visual chaos.
